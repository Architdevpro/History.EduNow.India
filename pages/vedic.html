Vedic Period (1500 BCE – 600 BCE)

The Vedic Period marks the foundation of Indian civilization, during which the Vedas – Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, and Atharvaveda – were composed. These texts form the bedrock of Indian spiritual, cultural, and social life.

Society

The Vedic society was organized into varnas (social groups), with occupations and duties defined. Early society was more fluid but gradually became stratified. Women had a respected position in early Vedic times, participating in education and rituals.

Politics

The polity was tribal, led by chiefs known as rajas. Assemblies such as the Sabha and Samiti played roles in decision-making. Over time, hereditary kingship and state formation began to emerge.

Economy

The economy was largely pastoral in the early Vedic phase, later shifting towards agriculture in the Later Vedic period. Trade and the use of nishka (gold ornaments) as currency were known.

Religion & Culture

The Vedic religion centered around fire sacrifices (yajnas) dedicated to deities such as Indra, Agni, and Varuna. Hymns and chants formed an essential part of worship. Music, poetry, and oral traditions flourished.

Legacy

The philosophical ideas and rituals of this era laid the groundwork for Hinduism, while also influencing later schools of thought such as Buddhism and Jainism.
